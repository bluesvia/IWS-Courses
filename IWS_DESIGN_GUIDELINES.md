# IWS Design Guidelines

## 1. Brand Design Direction
- **Identity:** Premium British online school.
- **Tone:** Trustworthy, academic, modern, family-friendly, and aspirational.
- **Visual System:** Clean navy/white visual system that instills confidence and professionalism.
- **Approach:** Conversion-focused but not aggressive. The design should guide users naturally toward enrolment without feeling "salesy".

## 2. Colour Palette
- **Primary:** Navy (`#0a1776`). Used for primary buttons, important headings, and strong section backgrounds.
- **Backgrounds:** White (`#ffffff`) and off-white/slate-50 (`#f8fafc`) for clean, readable content areas.
- **Neutrals:** Supporting neutral greys (e.g., slate-200 for borders, slate-600/700 for body text) to provide structure without overwhelming the primary colors.
- **Accents:** Reserved for specific callouts or trust badges (e.g., subtle gold/orange for Cambridge highlights, if applicable).
- **Usage Rules:**
  - **CTAs:** Primary CTAs should always use the primary Navy to stand out against white backgrounds, or White to stand out against Navy backgrounds.
  - **Cards:** White backgrounds with subtle shadows and slate-200 borders.
  - **Badges:** Soft background colors (e.g., blue-50) with darker text for readability.

## 3. Typography
- **Headings:** Bold, authoritative, and clean (Display fonts). Should use tight tracking and proper leading.
- **Body Text:** Legible sans-serif (e.g., Inter), typically medium or regular weight, using slate-600 to slate-800 for optimal contrast without the harshness of pure black.
- **Buttons:** Bold, typically sans-serif, well-spaced to ensure legibility.
- **Badges:** Small, bold, often uppercase or title case, using tracking to improve readability at small sizes.
- **Course Cards:** Clear hierarchy—Year group and subject must be immediately visible.
- **Parent-Facing Copy:** Professional, reassuring, clear, and concise. Avoid jargon. Use active voice.

## 4. Spacing Principles
- **Section Padding:** Generous vertical padding (e.g., `py-16` to `py-24`) to separate distinct areas of content and allow the design to breathe.
- **Card Spacing:** Inside cards, use consistent padding (e.g., `p-6` or `p-8`).
- **Grid Gaps:** Standardized gaps (e.g., `gap-6` or `gap-8`) between cards to maintain rhythm.
- **Mobile Spacing:** Reduce horizontal padding slightly for mobile, but maintain comfortable vertical rhythm so elements don't feel cramped.
- **CTAs:** Ensure sufficient whitespace around buttons to draw the eye and prevent accidental clicks on touch devices.

## 5. Component Patterns
- **Hero Section:** High-contrast (often Navy background), clear headline, reassuring subheadline, prominent trust badges, and an integrated course selector.
- **Trust Badges:** Simple icons paired with concise, accurate text (e.g., "Cambridge International curriculum", "Ages 7–19").
- **Course Selector:** Clean, intuitive dropdowns integrated directly into the hero or search areas.
- **Course Cards:** Image-led, clear tagging (level, duration), distinct CTA, and precise course naming (e.g., "IGCSE Mathematics — Year 10").
- **Feature Cards ("Why Choose IWS"):** Icon-led, clear titles, and bulleted lists or short paragraphs emphasizing structured learning and expert materials.
- **CTA Buttons:** Rounded corners (e.g., `rounded-xl` or `rounded-full`), strong contrast, action-oriented text.
- **Newsletter Block:** Distinct from main content (often using the primary Navy), clear value proposition, simple input field.
- **Course Detail Modules:** Clean list or grid layouts detailing syllabus, duration, and structure without overwhelming the user.
- **Footer:** Trust elements, accreditations, and clear contact/legal links.

## 6. Image and AI Asset Generation Rules
- **Subject Matter:** Images must show *online learning*. Focus on laptop-based study, home, café, or flexible environments.
- **What to Avoid:** Do NOT use traditional physical classroom or whiteboard teaching imagery unless specifically required for a unique context.
- **Style:** Realistic, premium, natural photography style.
- **Vibe:** Students should look engaged, independent, and supported. The overall feel should be calm, aspirational, international, and academic.
- **Quality:** Avoid over-stylized, cartoonish, or generic AI-looking visuals. Textures and lighting should feel authentic.

## 7. Mobile Design Rules
- **Grids:** Stack cleanly (1 column on mobile).
- **CTAs:** Keep primary buttons visible and tappable (minimum touch target size).
- **Readability:** Scale down large headings gracefully, but maintain legible body text sizes (minimum 16px recommended).
- **Hierarchy:** Maintain the same logical progression of information from desktop to mobile. Ensure users don't lose context when scrolling.
